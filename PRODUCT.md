# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences. (1) Training coordinators and HR managers at oil & gas operators (PDO-class companies in Oman and the Gulf) evaluating SGS as a training partner: they need to feel rigor, scale, and traceability. (2) Prospective trainees (field technicians, fresh graduates) deciding whether to enroll: they need to feel the career arc, from classroom to rig floor.

## Product Purpose

SGS runs a training management platform: employees enroll in accredited courses, their progress is tracked end-to-end, and every trainee deployed to an oil company is monitored after placement. The landing page is the front door, it must convert oil-company partners and trainees by making the pipeline (enroll → train → certify → deploy → monitor) feel inevitable and premium. Success: demo requests from operators, enrollments from trainees.

## Brand Personality

Sunlit, precise, field-proven. The feel of a Gulf industrial site at golden hour: warm, vast, serious. Cinematic confidence without darkness, luxury through light, space, and craft rather than gloss.

## Anti-references

- Dark "crypto/SaaS" neon dashboards.
- Navy + safety-orange corporate oilfield template sites.
- Generic LMS marketing (icon cards in threes, stock handshake photos).
- Glassmorphism hero overlays, gradient text.

## Design Principles

1. Light is the luxury: warm ivory/sand surfaces, generous space, imagery shot at golden hour.
2. The pipeline is the story: every section advances enroll → train → certify → deploy → monitor.
3. Typography does the heavy lifting: enormous serif display lines revealed with masked motion.
4. Motion is choreographed, not decorated: scroll-linked reveals with inertia, exponential ease-outs, one idea per fold.
5. Proof over promise: numbers, named programs, and the tracking timeline carry credibility.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast on all text. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback (content visible without animation). Semantic landmarks and alt text written in brand voice.
